Waterfalls 

Many people have different recommended routes when it comes to traveling the Golden Circle. One stop on everyone's map is the Gullfoss waterfall. Gullfoss, meaning “Golden Falls” in Icelandic, is located in the Hvítá River in Southwest Iceland. Although it is not the largest waterfall in Iceland, it has two stages which makes it visually more dramatic than other falls in Iceland. Water travels 36 feet down the first cascade and 69 feet down the second cascade before reaching to bottom of the Gullfossgjúfur canyon. 

The Gullfoss waterfall also has hiking trails surrounding the waterfall, which makes it an extremely popular destination for casual and experienced hikers alike. Although sometimes paths may be closed due to icy conditions, the trails are open year-round. 

Although Gullfoss may be the “Golden Fall,” there are still multiple other must-see waterfalls along the Golden Circle, like the Seljalandsá Waterfall located in Rangárþing Eystra.  Overall, it is estimated there are over 10,000 waterfalls in Iceland
