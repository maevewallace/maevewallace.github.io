Iceland Landmarks and the "Golden Circle"

Located between the Greenland Sea and the North Atlantic Ocean, Iceland is a country known for its rich and historic culture, beautiful natural landmarks and scenery, and astounding nature.  

Iceland's uniqueness makes it a popular tourist destination for all demographics. For history buffs, the historic sites and landmarks in Reykjavik are a must-see. With the increase in tourism in the 2010s, shopping and entertainment options for all ages and interests have increased throughout the country. For those interested in nature, hiking, skiing/snowmobiling, geothermal pools, and camping under the northern lights, Iceland is the country for you. 

 As tourism grew in Iceland through the 2010s, many people started trying to find and document the best method to experience all of the must-see attractions in Iceland. Many people agree that the best way to make sure you get to see all of Iceland’s most prominent landmarks is to travel a path called the “Golden Circle.” 

Depending on your schedule, how you want to travel, and your interests, there are many different routes and stops you can make while traveling the Golden Circle. Throughout my website I have documented the most popular attractions that have a spot on every tourist's Golden Circle guide, the different methods of touring the path, and my personal experience traveling along the Golden Circle.
